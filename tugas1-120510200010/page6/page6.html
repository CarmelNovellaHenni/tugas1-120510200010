Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

This condition usually develops when a genetic problem affects the heart muscle. It tends to be an inherited condition. The walls of the muscle thicken, and contractions become harder. This affects the heartâ€™s ability to take in and pump out blood. In some cases, an obstruction can occur.
There may be no symptoms
Many people do not receive a diagnosis

However, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy can worsen over time and lead to various heart problems.

Anyone with a family history of this condition should ask for screening, as receiving treatment can help prevent complications. Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy is the main cause of cardiac death among athletes and people aged under 35 years, according to the AHA.
